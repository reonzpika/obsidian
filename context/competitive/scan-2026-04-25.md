## CRITICAL FLAG

**THREE competitors have shipped or announced native Medtech Evolution integrations. This is the most important finding in this scan.**

1. **Medtech Global (first-party): Medtech AI launched 23 February 2026** — a native, first-party AI scribe embedded directly in Medtech Evolution via the ALEX platform. Context-aware patient summaries, ambient scribe, SOAP notes, referral letters. Data stays in NZ. All content reviewed by clinician before write-back. Pricing not yet public.

2. **Echo-Health.ai** — integration with Medtech Evolution went live May 2025 (pre-scan window). Mobile version now in development as of early 2026. Ambient scribe that writes directly into relevant record sections without copy-paste.

3. **IntelliTek Health** — Clinical Virtual Assistant integrated with Medtech Evolution via ALEX API. Adds AI-generated task posting, vital sign posting, billing/reason code recommendations, and referral automation inside Medtech Evolution.

**Heidi Health does NOT appear to have a Medtech Evolution integration** as of April 2026. Its NZ integrations are Elixir and MediOffice. Lyrebird Health is exclusively integrated with Best Practice (AU).

---

## Executive summary

- Medtech Global launched its own first-party AI scribe (Medtech AI) on 23 February 2026, directly competing with any third-party integration play on Medtech Evolution. This is the single biggest strategic threat to ClinicPro's scribe positioning.
- The ALEX partner API remains open. Echo-Health and IntelliTek have both shipped integrations, confirming the technical pathway is viable and available to ClinicPro.
- Heidi Health is moving beyond scribe into a broader "AI care partner" category: acquired AutoMedica (UK clinical AI), launched Heidi Evidence with HealthPathways integration, and raised $65M Series B (Oct 2025, valuation $465M).
- Regulatory pressure is building in AU/NZ. TGA published updated AI SaMD guidance on 5 February 2026; NZ Medical Products Bill will regulate SaMD (Parliament 2026, in effect ~2030). Scribes that generate diagnoses/differentials will be classified as medical devices.
- Pricing models are bifurcating: incumbent standalone scribes are raising prices (Heidi Clinician plan now $150/month, up from $90/month), while GP-specific and EMR-native tools are competing on integration depth and workflow fit rather than price alone.

## Regulatory update

### Australia (TGA)

- **5 February 2026:** TGA published updated guidance on AI-enabled SaMD regulation.
- **Key rule:** A digital scribe that analyses or interprets clinical conversations (e.g. generates a diagnosis, differential, or treatment recommendation not explicitly stated by the clinician) is classified as a medical device and must comply with SaMD requirements including lifecycle management, change control, and clinical evidence obligations.
- **Scribes that only transcribe and format:** Not medical devices under current TGA framework.
- **March 2026:** University of Melbourne researchers and clinicians publicly called for enforceable regulatory standards for AI scribes, citing hallucination risks. Coverage in Medical Republic and MedicalXpress.
- Regulatory arbitrage window is closing. Any scribe with clinical decision support features needs a compliance pathway.

### New Zealand (Medsafe / Medical Products Bill)

- **Medical Products Bill** will regulate SaMD including AI for therapeutic purposes. Bill to be introduced to Parliament in 2026. In effect approximately 2030.
- Definition is internationally aligned. General clinical software and general-use AI excluded.
- Medsafe and Pharmac are themselves adopting AI chatbots internally.
- Health NZ has endorsed four AI scribes for public health use (Heidi is one).

## Pricing model shifts

- **Heidi Health raised prices in early 2026.** Clinician plan up from $90/month to $150/month annually. Signals confidence in retention and reduced price sensitivity in the market.
- **Lyrebird:** Free tier (50 consults/month) remains. Pro at $240/month.
- **Grounded Scribe (AU-built):** Five tiers from free to $99/month + GST. Competing on price at the lower end.
- **Market split forming:**
  - **Premium integrated layer:** $99-$240/month/provider, native EMR write-back, clinical quality claims.
  - **Budget/volume tier:** $0-$99/month, copy-paste or basic push, less EMR depth.
  - **Enterprise/health system:** Custom pricing, $20,000-$100,000+ annually.
- **Usage-based pricing** (per-consult, per-minute) remains a minority model. Most AU/NZ vendors are moving to subscription.
- **Freemium is a standard acquisition tool.** Heidi, Lyrebird, Grounded Scribe all offer free tiers. Lock-in comes from EMR integration depth, not price.

## Strategic implications for ClinicPro

1. **Medtech Global is now a direct competitor in the scribe layer.** Medtech AI launched 23 February 2026 with first-party, deeply embedded integration. ClinicPro cannot win on "we integrate with Medtech Evolution" alone. The differentiator must be richer: what ClinicPro does beyond scribing (billing, recalls, care gap, patient engagement, population health) that Medtech AI does not.

2. **The ALEX API remains open.** ClinicPro can still integrate via the same channel Echo-Health and IntelliTek used. The window has not closed. But two third-party AI scribe players are already live on ALEX, so ClinicPro needs a differentiated feature set to justify practice adoption alongside or instead of existing ALEX partners.

3. **Heidi is the dominant standalone scribe brand in AU/NZ** and is accelerating into decision support and agentic care. It does not integrate with Medtech Evolution. This is a structural gap ClinicPro can exploit: position as the Medtech Evolution-native intelligent layer, not a generic scribe.

4. **Regulatory tailwind for NZ-hosted, clinician-controlled integrations.** Medtech AI and Echo-Health both lead on NZ data residency. ClinicPro should match this explicitly.

5. **Pricing discipline matters.** The market is segmenting. GP practices are price-sensitive but are paying $150-$240/month for scribe tools already. ClinicPro's bundle (scribe plus other tools) needs a clear per-consultation or per-practice value story.

6. **Liability and hallucination risk is a live policy debate.** Any ClinicPro output that approaches a diagnostic suggestion will need a compliance pathway (TGA SaMD if in AU, Medical Products Bill if in NZ). Structure the product to stay on the correct side of the transcription-vs-decision-support line until regulatory strategy is established.
