## 1. Diagnosis: What's Not Working

### The Numbers (Mar 4 - Mar 25, 8 tracked posts)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Posts tracked | 8 (6 pipeline, 2 manual) |
| Average impressions | 707 (median: 378) |
| Total reactions | 89 |
| Total comments received | 20 |
| Total followers gained | 8 (2/week) |
| Total saves | 10 |
| Total reposts | 1 |
| Engagement rate | ~1.6% average |

At current growth rate (2 followers/week), reaching 500 followers takes **43 more weeks**.

### What Performed

| Post | Impressions | Reactions | Why it worked |
|---|---|---|---|
| ED AI Scribe (1,250 clinicians) | 1,456 | 5 | Specific NZ event, concrete number, timely |
| Medtech AI in Evolution | 1,405 | 26 | Named product launch, insider take |
| Review-only post (unknown) | 1,219 | 34 | Likely personal/authentic tone |

### What Flopped

| Post | Impressions | Reactions | Why it failed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Manual post | 82 | 2 | No pipeline quality, no Golden Hour |
| HealthPathways/Heidi | 215 | 0 | Generic topic, no event anchor |
| Health AI clinical docs | 361 | 3 | Abstract framing, no clinical specificity |

### Root Causes

1. **Content burnout.** Ryo doesn't enjoy writing about NZ primary care infrastructure in the current format. Forced content reads as forced content.

2. **Format penalty.** Every post is text-only. Carousels get **596% more engagement** than text posts in 2026. This alone is the single largest missed opportunity.

3. **No commenting strategy.** Golden Hour (6 automated pre-post comments) is the only engagement activity. Daily commenting on other people's posts is the #1 growth lever for small accounts and it's completely absent.

4. **Over-constrained voice.** The rule "must open with a named NZ clinical system and a named event" forces every post into the same pattern. Readers can predict the structure after 3 posts. Predictable = scroll past.

5. **Too many posts, not enough quality.** 3x/week means 12 posts/month. At 155 followers, each post competes with itself for the same small audience. User's own observation: weekly posts performed better.

6. **No newsletter.** LinkedIn newsletters bypass the algorithm entirely. 100% of subscribers get notified. This is the highest-ROI channel for a small account and it doesn't exist.

7. **360Brew mismatch.** LinkedIn's 360Brew algorithm (deployed 2025) reads your profile headline + experience + post history to determine if you're a credible voice on your topic. If profile doesn't match content, distribution gets suppressed. Current profile may not be optimised for this.

## 2. The Pivot: Summary

**From:** 3 posts/week, text-only, infrastructure commentary, no outbound engagement
**To:** 3 posts/week (1 carousel + 2 text), fortnightly newsletter, daily 15-min commenting

**Core positioning shift:** From "NZ primary care infrastructure commentator" to **"The GP who builds."** Ryo's unique value is being a practising GP who also writes production software. The content is audience-value-first: every post must pass the test "Would a GP or practice owner in Christchurch, who has never heard of me, save or share this?" The builder identity shows up as a lens (why you have credibility), not as content. Builder reflections live in the newsletter.

## 3. New Content Pillars

### Pillar A: "What Works in NZ Primary Care" (55% of posts)

**The save-worthy pillar.** Practical knowledge: tools, workflows, technology decisions, things that actually help practices run better.

**Post types:**
1. **Tool Review** - "I tested Heidi AI scribe in my practice for 4 weeks. Here's the honest verdict."
2. **Workflow Tip** - "How I cut 30 minutes off my morning inbox routine. Three changes, no new software."
3. **Comparison** - "Heidi vs Nabla vs manual notes: AI scribes compared from a GP who's tried all three."
4. **Checklist / Framework** (carousel) - "7 questions to ask before your practice buys any health tech."
5. **Practice Hack** - "The Medtech shortcut most GPs don't know about."
6. **What Other Practices Do** - "I talked to 10 practice managers this month. The 3 tools they swear by."

**Why it works:**
- GPs and practice owners save practical content they can use later
- Checklists and comparisons have the highest save rate on LinkedIn
- Positions Ryo as someone who tests things in real practice, not just comments on them
- Sustainable: new material comes from daily practice

### Pillar B: "What's Changing in NZ Primary Care" (40% of posts)

**The share-worthy pillar.** Timely insight: what's shifting, what it means for practices, what to prepare for.

**Post types:**
1. **News Impact** - "PHOs are pooling AI procurement. Here's what it actually means for your practice."
2. **Policy Explainer** - "New pharmacist prescribing rules. What changes for GPs, starting next month."
3. **Myth-Buster** - "Everyone says AI scribes save GPs time. Here's what actually happens in the first 3 months."
4. **Trend Signal** (carousel) - "3 things I'm watching in NZ primary care this quarter and why they matter to your practice."
5. **System Truth** - "Why practices resist digital tools. It's not technophobia. It's rational self-defence."

**Why it works:**
- People share content that validates their experience or tells their network something important
- News impact posts ride timeliness for algorithm boost
- Myth-busters drive high comment counts (people agree or disagree)
- Google Alerts curation feeds this pillar directly

### 5% Flavour: Cross-Pillar / Personal

Occasional identity or reflection posts. Not planned, not a pillar. Used when something genuine comes up.

### Where "Building" Lives: The Newsletter

The main feed is 100% audience-value. The builder identity shows up as a lens (why Ryo has credibility to say this), not as content. Building reflections, product decisions, R&D learnings, and founder journey go in the newsletter.

### The Quality Gate

Every post must pass: **"Would a GP or practice owner in Christchurch, who has never heard of me, save or share this?"** If no, it doesn't go on the feed.

## 5. Format Mix

### Carousels (1x/week, Tuesday)

**Why:** Carousels are the #1 performing format on LinkedIn in 2026.
- 596% more engagement than text-only
- 278% more engagement than video
- 7.0% average engagement rate (vs 4.0% for text)
- Each swipe counts as engagement, boosting dwell time
- High save rate (saves drive 35% more secondary distribution)

**Specifications:**
- 6-8 slides (optimal for completion rate)
- 1080x1350px (portrait) or 1080x1080px (square)
- Export as PDF
- Large text (28px+), ample white space
- Use the existing ClinicPro visual design language (coral accent, DM Sans, Kalam headlines)
- First slide must stop the scroll: bold claim or surprising observation
- Last slide: key takeaway + subtle CTA (follow for more, not engagement bait)

**Carousel topic mapping to pillars:**
- Pillar A: "7 questions to ask before your practice buys any health tech"
- Pillar A: "What actually happens when your GP sends a referral (and where it breaks)"
- Pillar B: "3 things I'm watching in NZ primary care this quarter"
- Pillar B: "AI scribes compared: what a GP found after testing 3 options"

### Text Posts (1x/week, Thursday)

**Why:** Fast to produce, good for timely takes, strong when writing is sharp and personal.

**When to use text:**
- Timely NZ health event worth commenting on
- A single sharp observation from clinic that week
- A learning from building that doesn't need visual support

**Structure (loosened from current rules):**
- Hook: specific, personal, concrete (but no longer required to name a system)
- Body: 1-2 short paragraphs building the insight
- Close: a claim or position (not a question, not a summary)
- 150-250 words (tighter than current 150-300)

### Newsletter (fortnightly, Friday)

See Section 7 below.

## 6. Growth Engine: Daily Commenting

**This is the single highest-ROI change in the entire strategy.**

### Why Commenting Matters More Than Posting at 155 Followers

- A well-placed comment on a post with 10,000 impressions reaches more people than your own post
- Comments are weighted 15x more than likes in LinkedIn's algorithm
- One practitioner tripled profile views in 8 weeks with daily commenting alone
- 10-15 thoughtful comments/day generates 50-100 profile visits/day
- Each comment is a networking touchpoint with the post author

### The Protocol

**Time commitment:** 15 minutes/day, 5 days/week (Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri)

**Targets (build a list of 20-30 accounts to rotate through):**

| Category | Examples | Why |
|---|---|---|
| NZ GP leaders | Dr Bryan Betty, Dr Dee Mangin, Dr Angela Lim | Peer credibility |
| NZ health tech | HiNZ, Digital Health Association, Orion Health alumni | Industry visibility |
| Practice management | ProCare, Pinnacle, WellSouth PHO staff | Target audience |
| Health tech founders (AU/NZ) | Heidi Health, Eucalyptus, Tend Health | Peer network |
| NZ policy/health system | Health NZ comms, Ministry of Health, RNZCGP | Sector presence |

**Comment rules:**
- 15-30 words minimum (below 15 = not counted as "substantive" by algorithm)
- Must reference something specific in the post (not "Great post!")
- Lead with your own observation or experience, then connect to their point
- Comment within 30 minutes of target posting (early comments get most visibility)
- One comment per target per day maximum (don't stalk)

**Comment templates (adapt, don't copy-paste):**
- "We're seeing this in Auckland too. [specific detail from your practice]."
- "This matches what I've found building for GPs. The gap between [X] and [Y] is where the real friction is."
- "Interesting framing. In primary care the version of this is [specific example]."

**What to avoid:**
- "Great post!" / emoji-only reactions
- "What's your experience?" / engagement bait
- 50+ comments/day (360Brew detects and penalises)
- Engagement pods
- Generic comments that could apply to any post

## 7. Newsletter Strategy

### Why a Newsletter

- Bypasses the feed algorithm entirely: 100% of subscribers get email + push + in-app notification
- Regular posts reach ~5-7% of followers. Newsletters reach 100% of subscribers.
- LinkedIn auto-invites new followers to subscribe (compounding growth for free)
- Average open rate: ~40%
- Creates a owned-audience channel independent of algorithm changes
- Almost zero competition in NZ GP/health tech newsletter space

### Proposed Newsletter

**Name options (pick one):**
1. "From the Clinic Floor" (matches Pillar A, clinical grounding)
2. "The GP Builder" (captures dual identity)
3. "Primary Care Unpacked" (broader appeal to healthcare leaders)

**Frequency:** Fortnightly (every other Friday at 12:00 PM NZST)
- Weekly is optimal for growth but fortnightly is more sustainable alongside 2 posts/week
- Can increase to weekly after 3 months if it feels manageable

**Format (500-800 words):**
1. **The Week in NZ Health** (~200 words): Curated news digest from Google Alerts and industry sources. What happened and why it matters for practices.
2. **What I'm Building and Why** (~200 words): Builder reflections, product decisions, R&D learnings. This is where the founder identity lives (not on the main feed).
3. **One Thing Worth Trying** (~150 words): A specific tool, workflow tip, or resource for practices. Practical, save-worthy.

**Why this format works:**
- Section 1 provides curation value (readers come back because you surface things they'd miss)
- Section 2 builds the builder brand (without being promotional on the main feed)
- Section 3 reinforces "What Works" authority with practical recommendations
- Total read time: 3-4 minutes (optimal for newsletter retention)
- The main feed stays 100% audience-value. The newsletter is where the builder identity and news roundup live.

## 8. Profile Optimisation

**360Brew reads your profile to validate content authority.** If your headline says "GP" but your posts discuss health tech architecture, distribution gets suppressed.

### Recommended Profile Updates

**Headline (120 chars max):**

Proposed: `GP building software to fix NZ primary care | Founder, ClinicPro | MBIE R&D`

**Why:** Matches both pillars. 360Brew sees "GP" + "building software" + "primary care" and connects this to your post topics.

- Line 2-3: The problem you care about ("Every day I see the gap between what GPs need and what our systems provide.")
- Line 4-5: What you're doing about it ("I'm building ClinicPro to close that gap, funded by MBIE's New to R&D programme.")

**Creator Mode:** Must be ON (prioritises content distribution for small accounts).

**Featured section:** Pin your best-performing post (Medtech AI, 1,405 impressions) and the newsletter once launched.

## 11. Backup Plans

### If carousels don't outperform text after 4 weeks

**Diagnosis:** Check completion rates. If people swipe 2 slides and leave, the content isn't holding attention.
**Fix:** Reduce to 5-6 slides. Make each slide a standalone insight. Test square (1080x1080) vs portrait format.
**Fallback:** Replace carousel slot with a multi-image post (second-highest format at 6.6% engagement).

### If commenting doesn't drive profile views after 4 weeks

**Diagnosis:** Comments may be too generic or targeting wrong accounts.
**Fix:** Narrow targets to 10 accounts with 1,000-10,000 followers (large enough for visibility, small enough that your comment isn't buried). Comment within 15 minutes of their post.
**Fallback:** Shift commenting time to include LinkedIn group participation (NZ Health IT, RNZCGP, GP communities).

### If newsletter subscribers stay below 30 after 6 editions

**Diagnosis:** Topic or frequency mismatch.
**Fix:** Survey subscribers (short LinkedIn poll) on what they want more/less of. Test switching from fortnightly to weekly.
**Fallback:** Pause newsletter and redirect effort to a LinkedIn article series (similar reach mechanism, lower commitment).

### If engagement rate drops below 1% consistently

**Diagnosis:** 360Brew may not be associating your profile with your content topics.
**Fix:** Audit profile headline, about section, and experience for topic alignment. Ensure every post hits one of the 2 pillars. Post nothing off-topic for 30 days.
**Fallback:** Do a "content reset" by going silent for 1 week, then return with a strong carousel that re-establishes positioning.

### If Ryo burns out on 2 posts/week

**Reduce to 1 post/week + newsletter.** The commenting strategy alone drives more growth than posting at this follower count. One great carousel per week + daily commenting + fortnightly newsletter is still a strong strategy.

### 15.1 Competitive Landscape: The Space is Empty

**No NZ GP does personal thought leadership on LinkedIn.** No NZ healthcare LinkedIn newsletter exists.

**Implication:** The gap isn't differentiation. It's showing up first. "The GP who builds" is a literally unoccupied position in NZ LinkedIn.

**Comment target list (from research):**
- NZ tier: HiNZ, DHA, RNZCGP, ProCare, Health NZ, Angela Lim, Cecilia Robinson, Sophie Ball, Bryan Betty
- AU tier: Tom Kelly (Heidi), Aengus Tran (Harrison.ai), Heidi Health company page
- Global tier: Eric Topol, Bertalan Mesko, KevinMD (for visibility)

**Opportunity:** Digital Health Week 2026 (November, Auckland, HiNZ + DHA). Target speaking slot.

### 15.2 AI Content Detection: Healthcare Penalty is Worse

LinkedIn's 360Brew algorithm penalises AI content through quality signals, not binary detection. But **healthcare posts specifically suffer 44% less engagement when AI-generated** (Originality.ai, 3,368 posts, 2025), worse than the generic 30%.

**AI-tell patterns to avoid:**
- Em dashes (10x overuse rate vs human writing)
- Vocabulary: "delve", "tapestry", "landscape", "robust", "pivotal", "leverage", "seamless", "beacon", "realm"
- Phrasing: "In today's fast-paced world", "It's worth noting", "Let's dive in"
- Structural: uniform paragraph lengths, predictable transitions, zero personality

**Mandatory human editing protocol (10-15 min per post):**
1. "Only I could write this" test: add one personal detail (patient interaction, NZ-specific reference, practice metric)
2. Voice test: read aloud, rewrite anything that sounds like a press release
3. Structure test: vary paragraph lengths, add one sentence fragment, add one question
4. Vocabulary sweep: search-and-destroy the AI word list above
5. Opening test: kill any generic preamble
6. Opinion test: does this take a clear position? Add your actual view.
7. Specificity test: replace generic claims with concrete NZ examples

**Key insight:** AI-assisted posts (human edits the draft) perform comparably to human-written (~6.85% vs ~6.22% engagement). The penalty hits fully AI-generated posts only.

### 15.3 Carousel Design Specifications (Tightened)

| Spec | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Optimal slide count | **7** (18% better than any other length) | PostUnreel, 2026 |
| Format | Portrait **1080x1350** only | 25-35% more swipes than square |
| Words per slide | **20-30** (34% higher swipe-through vs 40+) | Multiple sources |
| Min font size | **32px body** (mobile readability) | 57% of LinkedIn traffic is mobile |
| Hook slide | Max **8-12 words**, profile photo, contrasting colour | 2.8x more clicks with unique first slide |
| Completion rate threshold | **35% minimum** or algorithm penalises | LinkBoost, 2026 |
| Upload format | **PDF only** (LinkedIn removed native carousel) | Platform change 2025 |
| Production time | **15-20 min** with Canva template | After one-time template setup |

**Carousel template (7 slides, portrait):**

| Slide | Purpose | Design |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Hook | Coral background, bold headline (8-12 words), subtitle, profile photo |
| 2 | Context/setup | White background, headline + 2-3 lines |
| 3-6 | Core content (1 point/slide) | White, numbered, 20-30 words each |
| 7 | CTA | Coral background, save/comment/follow prompt |

**Best carousel structures for healthcare:** Numbered listicle, myth-busting, step-by-step process, before/after, data highlight.

### 15.4 Video: Add Fortnightly, Don't Replace Carousels

**Recommendation:** 1 video per fortnight, replacing one Thursday text post (even weeks). 30-60 seconds, phone-recorded, from your office. One take. No editing beyond trimming.

**Why:**
- Zero NZ GP video competition (empty lane)
- Format diversity gives **37% more follower growth** and 28% more consistent visibility
- Talking-head video builds trust that text/carousels cannot (direct eye contact)
- Authentic phone video outperforms polished production on LinkedIn
- Comparable time investment to a text post (~15-30 min)

**Updated monthly output:** 4 carousels + 2 text posts + 2 videos + 2 newsletter editions

**Equipment:** Clip-on lapel mic ($15-25), phone tripod ($15-20), optional ring light ($30). Total: $30-75 one-time.

**Pin one strong video to your Featured section** as a trust anchor for every profile visitor.

### 15.5 Content Production Workflow (2 hrs 15 min/week)

**Daily micro-habit: Capture + Comment (15-20 min/day)**
- Voice memo capture between patients (30 seconds, raw thought, Otter.ai or native Voice Memos)
- 15 min commenting on LinkedIn (mobile app, 3-5 substantive comments + reply to own post comments)

**Weekly batch session: Sunday evening or Monday morning (60-90 min)**
1. Process voice memos, pick 2 strongest observations (15 min)
2. AI draft from seed content + past posts for voice (20 min)
3. Human edit using the 7-step protocol above (15 min)
4. Carousel production in Canva template (15 min)
5. Schedule everything via LinkedIn native scheduler (10 min)

**Fortnightly newsletter add-on (30 min every other week):**
- Expand 2 best-performing posts into newsletter sections
- Add "Worth Reading" links from Google Alerts curation
- AI draft → human edit → publish

**"Minimum viable LinkedIn" fallback for bad weeks:**
- Non-negotiable: 3 comments/day (10 min)
- If time for one more thing: 1 text post from a voice memo
- Skip order: newsletter first, then carousels, then video. Comments and text posts are last to go.

**Tool stack:** Otter.ai (capture), Obsidian (content bank), Claude (drafting), Canva (carousels), LinkedIn native scheduler (scheduling).

## 16. The One-Sentence Strategy

**Post less, post better, comment more, start a newsletter.**

Everything else is implementation detail.
