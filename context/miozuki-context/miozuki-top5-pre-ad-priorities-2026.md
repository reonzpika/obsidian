# Miozuki — Top 5 Changes Before Scaling Ads
*April 2026*

Five actions addressing the biggest conversion gaps in order of execution speed. Do them in this order.

---

## Priority 1: Change the Hero Headline Today
**Effort: 30 minutes. Impact: Immediate.**

**Why it's first:**
Every person who arrives from an ad and reads "Modern Diamond Alternatives in NZ" has been pre-framed as a bargain hunter. This is a 30-minute fix that is actively costing conversions right now.

**What to change:**

H1 (current): "Modern Diamond / *Alternatives in NZ*"

H1 options:
- "Fine jewellery inspired by the moon."
- "Chosen, not gifted. Fine jewellery for your actual life."
- "Born from a fortune slip in Fukuoka. Made for New Zealand."

Subheading: Keep "Moissanite and pearl fine jewellery, designed in New Zealand." — it works as a descriptor beneath an identity headline.

CTA: Keep "Shop Now" or soften to "Discover the Collection."

**What to remove everywhere above the fold:**
- "Diamond alternative" or "alternatives to diamond"
- "Modern diamond"
- Any language that anchors moissanite to diamond in a comparison frame

Move comparison language to the FAQ and future education content (blog, gemstone guide) where it serves informed-choice SEO. Not the homepage.

---

## Priority 2: Add Trust Signals to Every PDP Today
**Effort: Afternoon. Impact: Immediate.**

**Why it's second:**
No photography, no reviews, ads are running. The PDP is where conversions happen and die. Two copy changes close most of the gap without waiting for a photoshoot.

**Change A — Trust strip, immediately after the Add to Cart button:**

Add a row of four trust signals directly below the cart button on every product page:

```
✓ Free NZ shipping on orders over $150
✓ 30-day returns
✓ Lifetime warranty
✓ Secure checkout
```

Baymard research: 17% of cart abandonment comes from poor trust signals on the product page. After the cart button is exactly where abandonment anxiety peaks.

**Change B — Stone data in the Materials accordion:**

Add to every moissanite product description:

> Mohs hardness: 9.25 · Refractive index: 2.65 · Colour grade: [DEF equivalent] · Cut: [grade if available] · Certified by [AGS/IGI].

Hard numbers anchor quality perception and address the "is this real?" objection with facts. Avoid GRA certificates entirely — the Gemological Research Association is a documented scam with 1,200+ BBB complaints in 2023.

Both changes are pure copy and layout — no photography required. Done in an afternoon.

---

## Priority 3: Fix the Shipping Model
**Effort: Two hours including pricing review. Impact: Immediate.**

**Why it's third:**
68% of NZ cart abandonment is caused by delivery costs — this is the number one cause specifically in the NZ market (NZ Post 2025). 81% of NZ shoppers cite free delivery as a key purchase factor. At Miozuki's average order value ($255-$458 NZD), absorbing shipping above a threshold has a larger immediate conversion impact than most trust badge optimisations.

**What to change:**

1. Offer free shipping on orders over $150 NZD. If this requires a $5-8 price adjustment on products, apply it. The conversion lift will exceed the margin cost at this AOV.

2. Update announcement bar to: "Free NZ shipping on orders over $150 | $8 flat rate below."

3. Verify Afterpay is properly integrated at Shopify checkout. If live: add "or 4 payments of $X with Afterpay" below the price on every PDP. This is expected by NZ buyers under 45 — it is not a differentiator, it is table stakes. 77% of NZ BNPL users are under 45.

4. Update the PDP trust strip (from Priority 2) to reflect the new shipping threshold.

## Priority 4: Book the Photography Shoot
**Effort: 1-2 weeks to execute. Impact: Removes the conversion ceiling.**

**Why it's fourth:**
This is the highest-effort item but there is no ceiling on conversion improvement until it is done. AI-generated imagery on a product where "does it look real?" is the primary objection is a compounding trust deficit that no copy change can fully offset. The full-height "Accessible Luxury" hero image is specifically called out in the trust research as a significant risk.

**What to commission:**

Per hero SKU — prioritise 3-5 best sellers first:
- 2 studio shots (white background, angled)
- 2 on-model shots (scale reference, diverse skin tones)
- 1 macro detail close-up in natural light
- 1 sparkle loop video: 10-15 seconds, stone rotating in natural daylight showing rainbow fire

The sparkle video is non-negotiable for moissanite. Shoppers are 144% more likely to add to cart after seeing a product video (Picup Media). No written description of refractive index substitutes for seeing the fire move.

**Brief to photographer:**
Olivia & Pearl invested £3,500 in product photography (including on-body and scale shots) and saw £14,000 in incremental revenue within 6 months plus a 40% drop in return rates. That is the business case.

Start with the one product you want the most reviews on — that product becomes the trust anchor for the full catalogue.

---

## Priority 5: Get Five Reviews on One Product
**Effort: Ongoing from first order. Impact: 270-380% purchase likelihood increase.**

**Why it's fifth:**
Five reviews produce a 270% purchase likelihood increase (Spiegel Research Center). For Miozuki's price range, the lift is 380%. This is the final piece of the pre-scale trust stack.

**The method:**

Personal founder email to every buyer, sent 14-21 days post-delivery. Not automated — personal. 30-50% response rate versus 3-5% for automated email sequences.

Template structure:
1. Thank them specifically — name the piece they bought
2. Share something genuine about why you made it (the Fukuoka story works here)
3. Ask if they'd be willing to leave a photo review
4. Include the direct JudgeMe review link
5. No discount incentive needed for the first five — the personal ask is enough

**Why 14-21 days:**
Jewellery customers need time to receive, unbox, wear the piece in different settings, and receive compliments before they can write a meaningful review. Day 1 post-delivery is too soon.

**Where to concentrate reviews:**
One SKU — the hero product you photographed first. Once that product has 5+ reviews, the halo effect transfers across the catalogue. Six in ten shoppers will purchase a zero-review product from a brand where other products have strong ratings (PowerReviews).

**The star rating to aim for:**
4.0-4.7 stars — not 5.0. A perfect 5.0 decreases purchase likelihood due to suspicion of manipulation. Do not filter or curate early reviews.
