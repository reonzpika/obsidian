# Miozuki 2.0 — Website Changes for Your Review
*April 2026 — Ryo Nakamura*

---

## Why this document exists

We've done a full audit of the site across 14 domains, supported by five research reports on NZ consumer behaviour, fine jewellery DTC brand design, moissanite positioning, and trust architecture. The research is done. The findings are clear.

This document sets out what we change, why each change matters, and what we need your sign-off on before moving forward. I've split everything into two phases so you can approve what's urgent now and consider the bigger investments separately.

---

## The core problem

The site currently tells every new visitor that Miozuki is a "modern diamond alternative" — which permanently frames us as the budget option before they've seen a single product. This is the first thing every ad click reads.

This pattern repeats across the homepage hero, product pages, and navigation. Meanwhile we're running ads. Every click is landing on a site that actively works against the brand we're trying to build.

The good news: several of the highest-impact fixes are copy changes. No developer, no photoshoot, no new sprint. We can stop the bleeding this week.

## Phase 1: Quick wins

These are seven changes you can approve today. Each is a copy or settings change — no photography, no development work outside of Shopify theme editing.

---

### 1. Hero headline

**What's broken:** "Modern Diamond Alternatives in NZ" pre-frames every visitor as a bargain hunter before they see a product. Charles & Colvard used this kind of comparison positioning for a decade and filed for Chapter 11 in March 2026. The research is unambiguous: independent identity positioning outperforms comparison on every retention and premium metric.

**What changes:**

Current H1: *"Modern Diamond / Alternatives in NZ"*

Three replacement options — pick one, or suggest a variation:

- "Fine jewellery inspired by the moon."
- "Chosen, not gifted. Fine jewellery for your actual life."
- "Born from a fortune slip in Fukuoka. Made for New Zealand."

The subheading "Moissanite and pearl fine jewellery, designed in New Zealand" stays — it works as a descriptor beneath an identity-forward headline. Comparison language moves to the FAQ and education content, where it serves informed buyers without damaging first impressions.

---

### 2. Trust strip on every product page

**What's broken:** 17% of cart abandonment happens specifically at the Add to Cart button due to missing trust signals (Baymard Institute research). We have no trust strip anywhere on our product pages. Visitors who get to the cart have already decided they want the product — they abandon because anxiety peaks at the commit point.

**What changes:** Add four lines directly below the Add to Cart button on every product page:

```
✓ Free NZ shipping on orders over $150
✓ 30-day returns
✓ Lifetime warranty
✓ Secure checkout
```

This is a copy/layout change. One afternoon.

---

### 3. Stone data in the Materials accordion

**What's broken:** Our primary objection from moissanite buyers is "is this real?" We have no hard data on any product page to answer it. The stone's credentials are sitting in our FAQ, not where the purchase decision happens.

**What changes:** Add to every moissanite product description (in the Materials accordion):

> Mohs hardness: 9.25 · Refractive index: 2.65 · Colour grade: DEF equivalent · Certified by AGS/IGI.

One important note: do not reference GRA (Gemological Research Association). It is a documented scam — over 1,200 BBB complaints in 2023. AGS and IGI only.

---

### 4. Free shipping threshold + Afterpay badge

**What's broken:** 68% of NZ cart abandonment is caused by delivery costs — this is the number one specific cause in NZ (NZ Post 2025). 81% of NZ shoppers cite free delivery as a key factor. $8 flat rate on a $300 ring is costing conversions directly.

**What changes:**
- Free shipping on orders over $150 NZD. If this requires a $5–8 price adjustment on products, apply it — the conversion lift will exceed the margin cost at our AOV.
- Add "or 4 payments of $X with Afterpay" below the price on every product page. 77% of NZ BNPL users are under 45. This is table stakes for our demographic, not a differentiator.
- Update announcement bar to: "Free NZ shipping on orders over $150 | 30-day returns."

---

### 5. Returns policy: 14 days → 30 days

**What's broken:** Benchmark brands — Vrai, Mejuri, Monica Vinader — all offer 30-day returns minimum. Monica Vinader offers 100 days. NZ online shoppers expect at least 30 days for fine jewellery, which takes time to receive, wear in different settings, and evaluate.

**What changes:** Extend the returns window to 30 days. Policy text change only. No development work, no shipping cost change.

---

### 6. Navigation restructure

**What's broken:** "Moissanite FAQ" in primary navigation signals that we expect visitors to be sceptical before they've seen a product. "Shop All" hides category discovery behind a click. No Gifts link means we're missing one of the highest-intent purchase occasions. Industry data: "Gifts" appears in primary navigation for 4/5 fine jewellery benchmark brands.

**What changes:**

Current nav: *Best Sellers | Shop All | Moissanite FAQ | About*

New nav: **Rings | Earrings | Pearl Jewellery | Bridal | Best Sellers | Gifts**

Move to footer: About Miozuki, Our Founder, Moissanite FAQ, Care Guide, Shipping, Returns, Contact.

Optional: embed "Learn About Moissanite" as a link inside the Rings dropdown — visible at the decision point, not as a defensive top-level item.

---

### 7. NZ trust signals

**What's broken:** NZ Post 2025 data shows 29% of NZ shoppers cite "NZ-based seller" as a top purchase motivator — second only to discounts (46%). This is our strongest competitive advantage against offshore moissanite sellers and we're under-deploying it.

**What changes:**
- Add to footer: NZBN number, NZ city/suburb at minimum, "Proudly NZ-owned."
- Add to announcement bar rotation: "NZ-owned and operated | Ships from Auckland."
- Add "NZ-based" as one of three callout icons in the homepage differentiator section (alongside lifetime warranty and moissanite quality).

## Phase 2: Bigger investments

These seven items require planning, budget, or more time. They're the changes that remove the conversion ceiling — Phase 1 stops the bleeding, Phase 2 is how we scale.

I'm flagging them here so we're aligned on what comes next, not asking for approval on all of them today.

---

### 1. Product photography + sparkle videos

**What's broken:** AI-generated imagery for a product where the primary objection is "does it look real?" is a compounding trust deficit. No written copy can substitute for seeing the stone's fire move. Shoppers are 144% more likely to add to cart after seeing a product video (Picup Media).

**What changes:** Commission a professional photography shoot, starting with 3–5 best-selling SKUs. Per product: 2 studio shots (white background), 2 on-model shots (diverse skin tones for scale reference), 1 macro detail close-up, 1 sparkle loop video (10–15 seconds, stone rotating in natural daylight showing rainbow fire).

**Business case:** Olivia & Pearl, a UK jewellery brand, invested £3,500 in product photography including on-body shots, and saw £14,000 in incremental revenue within 6 months plus a 40% drop in return rates.

This is not optional before scaling ad spend. It's the single highest-impact investment.

---

### 2. Homepage architecture reorder

**What's broken:** The founder story currently appears at the third scroll position — before the first product grid. No benchmark fine jewellery brand places founder narrative this high. Mobile visitors from ads arrive expecting to see jewellery and get brand philosophy instead. No social proof of any kind exists on the homepage.

**What changes:**

Current order: *Hero → Brand Story → Collections → Best Sellers → FAQ → Footer*

New order: Hero → Collections grid → Best Sellers → Social proof bar → Differentiator icons → Email capture → Footer

The Fukuoka fortune slip story is genuinely compelling. It belongs on the About page where people actively choose to learn about the brand — not as the second thing every ad visitor sees.

---

### 3. Email capture

**What's broken:** No email capture exists anywhere on the site. Email is the lowest-cost retention channel for an early-stage brand. We're getting traffic and collecting nothing from visitors who don't convert on the first visit.

**What changes:** Add above footer — one field only (email address, nothing else — multi-field forms sharply reduce submission rate). Offer: early access to new drops, a free "Moissanite: The Honest Guide" PDF, or 10% off first order.

---

### 4. Cross-sell: "Complete the Look"

**What's broken:** No cross-sell section exists on any product page. Industry data shows 20–30% AOV increase from curated cross-sell (Monica Vinader, Stone and Strand). We have the catalogue depth to do this — we're just not using it.

**What changes:** Add 3 editorially curated complementary pieces above reviews on every product page. For a ring: pearl earrings + moissanite necklace. For pearl earrings: pearl necklace + moissanite ring. Manual curation, not algorithmic — the editorial feel is part of the brand.

---

### 5. Expert gemologist endorsement

**What's broken:** We have zero reviews. Academic research (Wang 2005, Journal of Advertising Research) shows that expert endorsements are more effective than customer reviews for building initial purchase confidence with an unfamiliar brand. A single credentialed quote can improve conversion by up to 400% in high-anxiety purchase categories (CRO research, Angie Schotmuller).

**What changes:** Secure one named, credentialed gemologist or jewellery professional — NZ-based preferred. A quote with a photo on the homepage and PDPs. This costs nothing beyond a relationship. It does more trust work than five early reviews at this stage.

---

### 6. Founding customer review programme

**What's broken:** Five reviews produce a 270% purchase likelihood increase (Spiegel Research Center, Northwestern University). For our price range, the lift is 380%. We need to get to five reviews on one product as fast as possible.

**What changes:** Personal founder email to every buyer, sent 14–21 days post-delivery. Not automated. Personal founder emails achieve 30–50% response rates versus 3–5% for automated sequences. The delay matters — buyers need time to receive, unbox, wear the piece in different settings, and receive compliments before they can write a meaningful review.

Concentrate all early reviews on one hero SKU. Once that product has 5+ reviews, the halo effect transfers across the catalogue — six in ten shoppers will purchase a zero-review product from a brand where other products have strong ratings (PowerReviews).

Target star rating: 4.0–4.7. Not 5.0. A perfect 5.0 decreases purchase likelihood due to suspicion of manipulation. Don't curate or filter early reviews.

---

### 7. Content strategy: moissanite + pearl hubs

**What's broken:** "Moissanite ring NZ" draws 480 searches per month in NZ with a difficulty score of 7 — very low competition. "Moissanite is what" draws 170/month, confirming many NZ buyers are encountering the stone for the first time. No NZ DTC brand dominates pearl education SEO. We're missing easily capturable organic traffic.

**What changes:** Build two interconnected content clusters:

**Moissanite hub** (5 articles): What is moissanite → Moissanite vs diamond (honest comparison) → Moissanite vs lab-grown diamond → How to read a grading report → Moissanite care guide

**Pearl hub** (5 articles): Types of pearls → How to choose pearl earrings → Pearl buying guide NZ → How to style pearls in 2026 → Pearl care guide

Each article links internally to relevant product collections. Pearl content first — pearl buyers are style-focused, not sceptical. Pearl SEO builds domain authority that transfers to the higher-skepticism moissanite products.

## Copy voice guide (internal — Phase 1 deliverable)

These do/don't pairs apply to everything we write: homepage, PDPs, ads, emails.

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "Modern diamond alternative" | "Fine jewellery chosen for its own brilliance" |
| "Affordable luxury" | "Accessible luxury" / "considered value" |
| "Shop Now" | "Discover the Collection" |
| "Moissanite is..." | "Miozuki jewellery is set with moissanite..." |
| "Budget-friendly" | "Intentional" / "thoughtful" |
| "Cheaper than diamond" | "A stone chosen for its own properties" |

The rule: anchor the brand first, then the material. Never the reverse.
