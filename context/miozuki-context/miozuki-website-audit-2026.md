# Miozuki — Pre-Launch Website Audit
*April 2026 — Based on five research reports + live codebase audit*

Fourteen audit domains. Each rated Critical / High / Medium / Low based on evidence. Ordered by impact.

---

## Domain 1: Positioning & Hero Copy
**Rating: CRITICAL**

**Current state:**
Hero headline reads "Modern Diamond / *Alternatives in NZ*" with subheading "Moissanite & Pearl Fine Jewellery."

**What the research says:**
Comparison positioning permanently frames the brand as the budget option. Charles & Colvard led with it for a decade and filed for Chapter 11 in March 2026. The research is categorical: independent identity outperforms comparison on every retention and premium metric. Comparison language belongs in education content (blog, FAQ, gemstone guides) where it serves SEO and informed-choice framing — not on the homepage hero where it is the first thing every new visitor reads.

**The gap:**
The current hero is actively undermining the brand before a single product is seen. "Diamond alternative" signals budget, not design philosophy.

**Recommendation:**
Rewrite the hero around Miozuki's Japanese-origin story and design identity. The brand name itself (澪月, "waterway to the moon") is a compelling headline that no competitor could replicate. Options:

- "Fine jewellery inspired by the moon."
- "Chosen, not gifted. Fine jewellery for your actual life."
- "Born from a fortune slip in Fukuoka. Made for New Zealand."

The subheading can introduce moissanite and pearl without comparison framing: "Moissanite and pearl fine jewellery, designed in New Zealand." CTA: soften "Shop Now" to "Discover the Collection."

Eliminate all diamond-comparison language from the homepage, hero, and above-the-fold product copy. Move it to the education hub where it does its job.

## Domain 2: Product Photography
**Rating: CRITICAL**

**Current state:**
The trust research explicitly flags AI-generated imagery as a significant risk for Miozuki. The "Accessible Luxury" hero section image is specifically called out. No sparkle loop videos are present.

**What the research says:**
For moissanite specifically, the stone's rainbow fire must be demonstrated, not described. Shoppers are 144% more likely to add to cart after seeing a product video (Picup Media). On-model imagery increases conversion up to 33% and reduces returns by 22%. The minimum PDP image set is: two studio cut-outs, two on-model scale shots, one lifestyle image, one detail close-up, and one sparkle loop video.

For a product where the primary consumer objection is "does it look real?", AI-generated imagery is a compounding trust deficit. The visual proof that moissanite's fire is real cannot come from a render.

**The gap:**
AI-generated images directly contradict the brand's credibility proposition. Every conversion effort runs into this ceiling.

**Recommendation:**
Commission a professional product photography shoot before scaling ad spend. Minimum requirement per product:
- Two studio shots (white background, angled)
- Two on-model shots (diverse skin tones, scale reference)
- One lifestyle/context shot
- One macro detail of the stone in natural light
- One 10-15 second sparkle loop video (stone rotating in natural daylight)

Reference: The Olivia & Pearl case study — £3,500 photography investment returned £14,000 incremental revenue in 6 months and a 40% drop in return rates. This is not optional before scaling paid ads.

## Domain 3: Trust Architecture (Zero-Review State)
**Rating: CRITICAL**

**Current state:**
JudgeMe is integrated on PDPs. No reviews currently visible. No expert endorsements. No stone certifications displayed. No "As Featured In" press bar. No gemologist quote. No packaging photography on PDPs.

**What the research says:**
Products jumping from zero to five reviews see a 270% purchase likelihood increase (Spiegel Research Center). For higher-priced items in Miozuki's range, the lift is 380%. Expert endorsements outperform peer reviews at the pre-consideration stage — before reviews exist, a named credentialed expert quote carries more trust weight than five generic early reviews. Third-party stone certifications are "the primary source of confidence" for online jewellery buyers (GSI, 2025).

**The gap:**
Miozuki is running ads to a zero-proof site. Every visitor who doesn't convert is paying for that trust gap.

**Recommendation (in order of execution speed):**

1. Add a named gemologist or jewellery professional endorsement quote with photo to the homepage and every PDP. A single credentialed quote costs nothing beyond a relationship.

2. Display stone grading data on every PDP: Mohs hardness (9.25), refractive index (2.65 vs diamond's 2.42), cut grade, colour grade equivalent. Source from supplier certifications. Legitimate grading bodies only — AGS or IGI. Avoid GRA entirely (documented scam; 1,200+ BBB complaints in 2023).

3. Add packaging photography as a secondary PDP image. 80% of consumers trust unboxing content as much as personal recommendations.

4. Seed founding customer programme: personal founder email to every early buyer at 14-21 days post-delivery. Target first five reviews on one hero SKU. 30-50% response rate versus 3-5% for automated emails. Concentrate all early reviews on one product for halo effect — six in ten shoppers will buy a zero-review product from a brand with strong ratings elsewhere.

5. Secure one NZ press mention for an "As Featured In" bar. Target NZ Herald Viva, Denizen, Together Journal, or Fashion Quarterly gift guides. One small mention creates a legitimate credibility signal. Start outreach immediately; time for seasonal gift guides.

## Domain 4: Navigation Structure
**Rating: HIGH**

**Current state:**
Nav has four items: Best Sellers | Shop All (dropdown with subcategories) | Moissanite FAQ | About (dropdown).

**What the research says:**
Product categories should be individual top-level items, not collapsed under "Shop All." Educational content belongs in the footer, not primary navigation. "Gifts" appears in primary nav for 4/5 benchmark brands. "Moissanite FAQ" in primary nav signals that the brand expects visitors to be sceptical before they've seen a product.

**The gap:**
"Moissanite FAQ" as a primary nav item is defensive — it frontloads objection-handling before trust is established. "Shop All" hides category discovery behind a click. No Gifts link means a major purchase occasion goes unaddressed.

**Recommendation:**
Restructure desktop nav to:

**Rings | Earrings | Pearl Jewellery | Bridal | Best Sellers | Gifts**

Move Moissanite FAQ, About Miozuki, and Our Founder to footer. If education should be accessible from nav, embed a "Learn About Moissanite" link inside the Rings dropdown — visible at the decision point, not as a top-level commitment.

Footer: About Miozuki, Our Founder, Moissanite FAQ, Care Guide, Size Guide, Warranty, Contact, Shipping, Returns.

## Domain 5: Homepage Architecture
**Rating: HIGH**

**Current state:**
Section order: Announcement bar → Header → Hero → Brand Story (founder + origin) → Collections grid → Best Sellers → Accessible Luxury hero → FAQ → Footer.

**What the research says:**
Industry standard sequence: Hero → Collections/categories → Product grid → Differentiator section → Social proof → Email capture → Footer. No benchmark brand places founder narrative in the third scroll position — founder stories live on About pages. No social proof anywhere on homepage is a critical gap.

**The gap:**
Brand Story section appears before the first product grid. Mobile visitors from ads arrive expecting to see jewellery and get brand philosophy first. No press logos, no expert quote, no social proof of any kind on the homepage. No email capture exists.

**Recommendation:**
Reorder homepage:
1. Announcement bar (rotate trust signals)
2. Hero (new identity-forward copy)
3. Collections grid
4. Best Sellers product grid
5. Social proof bar ("As Featured In" press logos — even one mention qualifies)
6. Brand differentiator section (3 icons: NZ-based, moissanite, lifetime warranty)
7. Email capture (offer: early access or care guide PDF)
8. Footer

Move the founder origin story to the About page. The Fukuoka fortune slip story is genuinely compelling — it belongs where people are actively choosing to learn about the brand.

## Domain 6: Product Detail Page Information Hierarchy
**Rating: HIGH**

**Current state:**
Flow: Breadcrumb → Gallery → Product type label → Title → Price → Divider → Variant selectors → Engraving field → Add to Cart → Expandable sections → Reviews.

**What the research says:**
Validated hierarchy: Name/rating → Price (with BNPL mention) → Material swatches → Size buttons → Add to Cart → Trust strip → Short description → Collapsible accordions → Complete the Look → Reviews.

Three specific gaps:
1. No trust strip immediately after Add to Cart — 17% of cart abandonment from poor trust signals on PDP (Baymard).
2. No "Complete the Look" cross-sell — 20-30% AOV increase.
3. No sparkle video in gallery — for moissanite, this is the key objection resolution.

**Recommendation:**
Add immediately after Add to Cart button:
- ✓ Free NZ shipping on orders over $150
- ✓ 30-day returns
- ✓ Lifetime warranty
- ✓ Secure checkout

Add "Complete the Look" section above reviews — 3 editorially selected complementary pieces.

Add sparkle loop video as first or second gallery item.

Add stone grading data in Materials accordion: Mohs 9.25, RI 2.65, colour grade equivalent, AGS/IGI certification reference.

## Domain 7: Shipping & BNPL Strategy
**Rating: HIGH**

**Current state:**
$8 flat rate shipping on all orders. No free shipping threshold. Footer shows Visa, MC, Amex, Apple Pay, Google Pay, PayPal, Shop Pay. No visible Afterpay badge on PDPs.

**What the research says:**
81% of NZ shoppers cite free delivery as a key factor. 68% abandon carts specifically due to high delivery costs — the number one cause of cart abandonment in NZ. Afterpay: 77% of BNPL users are under 45, 19% BNPL transaction growth in 2024.

**The gap:**
$8 flat rate on a $300 ring adds psychological friction that outweighs its margin value. Afterpay not visible in the buy box means Miozuki is missing a critical conversion signal for the target demographic.

**Recommendation:**
Offer free shipping on orders over $150 NZD. Absorb the $8 into product pricing if needed — conversion lift will exceed margin cost.

Verify Afterpay integration at checkout. Add Afterpay badge to PDPs ("or 4 payments of $X with Afterpay") immediately below the price.

Update announcement bar: "Free NZ shipping on orders over $150."

## Domain 8: NZ Trust Signals
**Rating: HIGH**

**Current state:**
NZD pricing, $8 NZ shipping, NZ-based FAQ answer, NZ founder story. No NZBN visible. No NZ street address in footer. No explicit "NZ-owned" badge.

**What the research says:**
NZ Post 2025 data: 29% of shoppers cite NZ-based seller as a top purchase motivator — second only to discounts. 73% of online spend went to NZ-based businesses in 2022. This is Miozuki's strongest structural advantage against offshore moissanite sellers and it is currently under-deployed.

**Recommendation:**
- Add to footer: NZBN number, physical NZ address (or suburb/city minimum), "Proudly NZ-owned."
- Add to announcement bar rotation: "NZ-owned and operated | Ships from Auckland."
- Investigate Buy NZ Made certification (buynz.org.nz) — if any assembly or finishing occurs in NZ, the Kiwi trademark is a high-credibility signal.
- Add "NZ-based" as one of three icon callouts in the homepage differentiator section.

## Domain 9: Copy Voice Consistency
**Rating: MEDIUM-HIGH**

**Current state:**
Brand voice shifts register across the site:
- Hero: "Modern Diamond Alternatives in NZ" — functional, comparison
- Brand Story: "For women who choose meaning over tradition" — aspirational, identity
- FAQ: Educational, matter-of-fact
- Footer tagline: "Fine jewellery, designed in New Zealand. Inspired by the moon." — poetic

**The gap:**
The hero undermines every other voice signal. The four touchpoints don't feel like the same brand.

**Recommendation:**
Draft a one-page internal voice guide covering 5-10 do/don't pairs:
- "Modern Diamond Alternative" → "Fine jewellery chosen for its own brilliance"
- "Affordable luxury" → keep "accessible luxury"
- "Shop Now" → "Discover the Collection" (softer CTA for brand tier)
- "Moissanite is..." → "Miozuki jewellery is set with moissanite..." (anchor brand, then material)

Apply consistently across hero, PDPs, About page, and any ad copy.

## Domain 10: Content Strategy
**Rating: HIGH**

**Current state:**
Blog/journal exists. Moissanite FAQ page exists as a standalone. No interconnected content hub. No pearl education content found.

**What the research says:**
Two interconnected content hubs recommended: moissanite cluster and pearl cluster, each linking internally to product pages. NZ keyword opportunities: "moissanite ring NZ" (480/mo, SD 7), "moissanite is what" (170/mo), "moissanite vs diamond" (320/mo). Pearl content is the asymmetric advantage — pearl searchers are style-focused buyers, not sceptics, and no NZ DTC brand dominates pearl education SEO.

**Recommendation:**

**Moissanite hub** (launch first, highest search intent):
- "What is moissanite?" — captures "moissanite is what" 170/mo
- "Moissanite vs diamond: an honest comparison" — captures 320/mo
- "Moissanite vs lab-grown diamond"
- "How to read a moissanite grading report"
- "Moissanite care guide"

**Pearl hub** (lower scepticism, seasonal gifting traffic):
- "Types of pearls: freshwater, Akoya, Tahitian"
- "How to choose pearl earrings"
- "Pearl buying guide NZ"
- "How to style pearls in 2026"
- "Pearl care guide"

Each article links internally to relevant product collections. Use the existing moissanite-faq page as the anchor for the moissanite hub.

## Domain 11: Logomark & Brand Identity System
**Rating: MEDIUM-HIGH**

**Current state:**
"Miozuki" appears as styled text in the header and footer. No formal wordmark lockup, no horizontal/stacked/icon variations, no documented colour palette with Pantone/CMYK values, no brand guidelines.

**What the research says:**
A professional wordmark with lockup variations is non-negotiable before press outreach or wholesale. Editors and buyers assess this first. Press kits require logo files in multiple formats.

**Recommendation:**
Commission a brand identity formalisation (not a rebrand — the visual direction is already right). Deliverables:
- Wordmark in horizontal and stacked lockups
- Favicon/monogram mark (澪 character or stylised M)
- Colour palette with hex, RGB, and Pantone values documented
- Typography usage guide (Playfair Display + DM Sans is the right pairing — formalise it)
- One-page brand guidelines document

This enables press kit assembly and wholesale enquiries.

## Domain 12: Email Capture
**Rating: MEDIUM**

**Current state:**
No email capture found on the homepage or in site navigation.

**What the research says:**
Email/membership capture is step 8 in the industry-standard homepage section sequence. For an early-stage brand, email is the lowest-cost retention channel.

**Recommendation:**
Add email capture above footer with a relevant offer:
- Early access to new drops, or
- Free "Moissanite: The Honest Guide" PDF (content marketing + lead gen), or
- 10% off first order (maintains price integrity at launch, switch to access-based capture later)

One field only — email address. Multi-field forms reduce submission rate.

## Domain 13: Cross-Sell Architecture
**Rating: MEDIUM**

**Current state:**
No cross-sell section visible on PDPs.

**What the research says:**
"Complete the Look" curated cross-sell increases AOV 20-30%. Most effective pattern is 3-5 editorially selected complementary pieces, not algorithmic.

**Recommendation:**
Add "Complete the Look" section above reviews on every PDP. Manually curate 3 complementary pieces per product. For a ring: pair with pearl earrings and a moissanite necklace. For pearl earrings: pair with a pearl necklace and a moissanite ring. Showcases range depth without requiring more traffic.

---

## Domain 14: Returns Policy Duration
**Rating: MEDIUM**

**Current state:**
14-day returns policy.

**What the research says:**
Benchmark brands: Vrai 30-day, Monica Vinader 100-day, Mejuri 30-day. NZ online shoppers expect a minimum 30-day window for fine jewellery — a product that requires time to receive, try, and evaluate in different settings.

**Recommendation:**
Extend to 30 days minimum. Policy change only, no development required.
