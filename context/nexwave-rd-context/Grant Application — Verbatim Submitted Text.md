## R&D Activity

**Describe your planned R&D activities**

We are conducting systematic R&D to determine whether artificial intelligence can achieve clinical-grade performance (≥90% accuracy, ≥99% HIGH-urgency sensitivity) for triaging GP inbox items—laboratory results, discharge summaries, radiology reports, and specialist correspondence—across heterogeneous New Zealand general practice environments.

Our investigation progresses through four sequential phases addressing specific uncertainties:

STU-1: Clinical Data Structuring & Human Performance Baseline (Months 1-6)

Create labelled ground-truth dataset (500-1,000 NZ GP inbox items)
Measure inter-rater reliability amongst GP experts (Cohen's kappa)
Quantify data quality variance across Practice Management Systems (MedTech, Indici, myPractice)
Establish human performance ceiling determining if 90% AI accuracy is theoretically achievable
STU-2: Architecture Investigation & Performance Optimisation (Months 7-15)

Test fine-tuned medical LLMs, RAG systems, hybrid approaches, agentic workflows
Optimise patient history context (minimal vs. comprehensive longitudinal records)
Develop uncertainty quantification enabling clinical confidence thresholds
Determine which architecture(s) can achieve target performance on cleaned data
STU-3: Controlled Deployment & Stress Testing (Months 16-20)

Shadow-mode deployment across 2-3 diverse pilot practices
Quantify laboratory-to-field performance degradation
Identify failure modes through adversarial and edge case testing
Validate real-world accuracy ≥80%, HIGH-urgency sensitivity ≥99%
STU-4: Operational Deployment & Field Validation (Months 21-24)

Scale to 5-8 practices representing urban/rural, solo/group, diverse patient demographics
Monitor operational performance, GP override patterns, safety incidents
Analyse practice-context factors predicting deployment success
Establish practice-suitability criteria for commercial rollout
Outcome: Either validated clinical-grade AI meeting performance targets, or documented technical boundaries establishing maximum achievable performance for future research.

## Uncertainty Test

**What is the specific scientific or technological uncertainty that you face?**

Our R&D addresses four specific uncertainties that cannot be resolved without systematic investigation:

Uncertainty 1: Data Quality & Clinical Performance

Can AI achieve ≥90% accuracy and ≥99% HIGH-urgency sensitivity on heterogeneous, unstructured NZ GP inbox data without manual curation?

GP inbox items arrive in wildly varying formats:
Numeric lab tables vs. PDF scans vs. HL7 messages
200-word discharge notes vs. 5-page reports
Structured radiology templates vs. narrative descriptions
No existing system has demonstrated clinical-grade performance across this data heterogeneity spanning multiple incompatible Practice Management Systems
Milestone: At least one architecture achieves both performance thresholds simultaneously on validation dataset (STU-2)

Uncertainty 2: Real-World Performance Degradation

How much does AI performance degrade from laboratory validation to real GP practice deployment?

Clinical AI typically degrades 5-15 percentage points from controlled datasets to live settings
Magnitude for NZ GP contexts is unknown and varies unpredictably based on practice characteristics
Milestone: Quantify degradation magnitude; maintain real-world accuracy ≥85%, HIGH-sensitivity ≥97% (STU-3/4)

Uncertainty 3: Optimal Clinical Context

What patient history amount optimises AI accuracy versus introducing noise?

Research shows larger context windows can paradoxically degrade performance
No methodology exists for determining minimum sufficient patient history for GP inbox triage
Milestone: Identify optimal context depth (±20% accuracy variance) through systematic testing (STU-2)

Uncertainty 4: Human Performance Ceiling

Can inter-rater reliability amongst GPs establish achievable performance limits?

If expert GPs disagree on urgency classification, AI cannot exceed human agreement levels
Milestone: Measure Cohen's kappa ≥0.7 validating 90% accuracy is theoretically achievable (STU-1)

**Why is your scientific or technological challenge difficult to achieve for a professional?**

A competent professional in clinical machine learning cannot resolve these uncertainties without systematic investigation because:

No established methodology exists for this specific problem

No published approach addresses AI triage of heterogeneous GP inbox correspondence across multiple incompatible PMS systems (MedTech, Indici, myPractice) with varying data formats, completeness, and quality
The specific combination of NZ clinical correspondence types, PMS integration requirements, and clinical workflow constraints has not been systematically studied
The sensitivity-accuracy trade-off is unresolved

Published medical AI systems either:
Maximise sensitivity (catching urgent items but generating excessive false alarms), OR
Optimise accuracy (risking missed critical findings)
No documented approach achieves our combined requirements (≥99% sensitivity + ≥90% accuracy) on heterogeneous clinical correspondence
FDA-cleared triage devices achieve 84-96% sensitivity on narrow, specialised imaging tasks, not unstructured clinical documents
Laboratory performance does not predict real-world deployment

A 2024 Nature Communications study demonstrated clinical AI performance drops 8-25 percentage points when deployed across different healthcare settings
Whether any architecture can maintain clinical-grade performance on real NZ GP data with interruptions, time pressure, edge cases, and data quality variance cannot be predicted without field validation
The optimal context window is unknown

Research shows larger context can paradoxically degrade LLM performance as models become more generic
No guidance exists for optimal patient history length in clinical inbox triage
This interaction effect must be empirically determined

**What other solutions are you aware of that achieve a similar outcome?**

No dedicated GP inbox triage AI exists in New Zealand or internationally that addresses our specific problem.

NZ Market:

Clinical scribes (Heidi Health, iMedX, Echo-Health.ai) transcribe consultations but do not process inbox items
Health Accelerator's inbox tools are rule-based RPA (predefined filing steps), not AI capable of clinical judgment
PMS vendors (MedTech, Indici, myPractice) offer AI scribes but no inbox triage capabilities
International Systems: UK solutions (GP Triage, Rapid Health, Patchs) are purpose-built for NHS infrastructure and unavailable to us because:

They integrate exclusively with NHS systems (EMIS, SystmOne) using NHS-specific APIs. NZ uses completely different PMS systems
They are trained on UK clinical language, referral pathways, and guidelines (NICE), direct deployment would require complete retraining
Published research shows clinical AI suffers 8-25% performance degradation when deployed across different healthcare systems, making simple transfer non-viable
Academic Research: Published ED triage studies achieve 80-90% accuracy on hospital emergency scenarios, different data types, different clinical context, different workflows. No published research addresses GP inbox triage with our specific performance requirements.

Why we cannot use existing solutions: The combination of NZ PMS heterogeneity, clinical correspondence formats, and required performance thresholds (≥90% accuracy, ≥99% HIGH sensitivity) is not addressed by any available system. We must develop and validate a NZ-specific approach.
