## Features discussed

### 1. Inbox AI summarisation

**Concept presented:** AI reads incoming clinical letters and discharge summaries, produces a short summary so the GP doesn't read the full document unless needed.

**Nick's response:** Positive. Confirmed the inbox volume problem. Suggested a "levelling system" (appears to mean prioritisation or triage by urgency) should be part of the feature, not just summarisation.

> "There should be just a levelling system"

**Takeaway:** Summarisation alone is not enough. Priority/urgency triage is an expected part of the value proposition.

---

### 2. Lab result trend analysis

**Concept presented:** When a new lab result arrives, AI compares it to the previous result and adds a one-line comment (e.g., "improving", "stable", "worse") so the GP doesn't have to manually pull up the previous entry.

**Nick's response:** Strongly positive. Confirmed the manual lookup friction is real.

> "Usually you have to look at the previous, you want to just remind yourself"

**Extension discussed:** If a patient had a previous significant event (e.g., acute kidney injury), AI should flag that context when a new renal result arrives, not just compare numbers.

> "Kidney injury — do you think that would be helpful or do you find that really annoying?"
> Nick: positive/affirming

**Specific test mentioned:** Renal function (eGFR/creatinine). Also mentioned HPA (possibly hypothalamic-pituitary, or may be misheard from transcript).

**Takeaway:** Trend commenting + historical event flagging both validated. Priority feature.
