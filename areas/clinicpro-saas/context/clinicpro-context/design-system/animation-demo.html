Demo 01 Highest impact

Magnetic CTA button

The button physically pulls toward your cursor as it approaches, then springs back on leave. Spring physics — not a CSS transition. Almost never seen on clinical SaaS. Move your cursor slowly toward the button from outside the white box.

Demo 02 Highest impact

Word-by-word scroll reveal

Each word brightens as your scroll position reaches it — linked to scroll depth, not a timer. Used by Linear.app and Vercel. Makes copy feel spoken. Scroll slowly through the quote below to see it.

Demo 04 Strong signal

3D perspective card tilt

Cards tilt in 3D toward your cursor with a soft light-reflection that tracks mouse position. Rare on clinical SaaS. Gracefully degrades to standard shadow lift on mobile. Move your cursor slowly over each card.

№ 01

Multi-step transfer

Email to self → download → find file → resize → attach. Up to 30 min when things go wrong.

№ 02

File size rejections

Full-res phone photos are 3–8 MB. ERMS and HealthLink have strict limits.

№ 03

Wrong format

Specialists request JPEGs. Most tools produce PDFs. Back-and-forth delays the referral.

H1 Most popular

Line mask reveal

Each line of the headline is hidden behind an overflow:hidden mask. The text slides upward into view, line by line, staggered. Used on Stripe, Linear, Vercel. The most premium-feeling heading entry on the web right now.

Clinical photos.
On your desktop.

H2 Strong signal

Blur-to-focus reveal

Each word starts blurred and out-of-focus, then sharpens word by word. Metaphor for clarity emerging — fits a tool that eliminates a messy workflow. Subtle and clinical-feeling.

Clinical photos. On your desktop. In 5 seconds.

H3 Uncommon

Letter-spacing collapse

The headline starts spread wide — tracking at 0.5em — then collapses to tight. Combined with a fade-in. Very unusual, very elegant. Works best on a short, punchy single line. Almost never seen on clinical SaaS.

In 5 seconds.

H4 High craft signal

Character-by-character stagger

Each character rises individually from behind a hidden mask, with a tiny stagger between each. The text appears to "grow" across the screen. More complex than line-mask, but creates a very deliberate, precise feeling.

H5 Uncommon · Divisive

Scoreboard flip

Each character flips in on its X-axis — like an airport departures board revealing new information. Mechanical, precise, slightly terminal-like. Works well on section headings in the dark security section. Divisive: love it or skip it.

H6 Energetic

Kinetic word scale

Each word scales up from 0.6 to 1 and fades in, one at a time in rapid sequence. Creates forward momentum — good for a CTA-heavy hero. More energetic than line-mask; use for shorter headlines.
