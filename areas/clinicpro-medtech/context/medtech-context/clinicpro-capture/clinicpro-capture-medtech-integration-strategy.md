# Medtech Integration Contract: Internal Strategy Notes

Strategy for the ClinicPro × Medtech integration contract (commission on direct sales), covering both NZ and AU markets. Sits alongside the separate AU bundle deal. Both land at Wednesday 22 April 2026 meeting.

## 1. Strategic context

- **Medtech is the only PMS without a native image capture tool.** Indici, Bp Premier, Best Practice, MedicalDirector already have this solved. Medtech uniquely needs Capture to plug a competitive gap. BATNA inversion: they need us on this product specifically more than we need them.
- **Working with Medtech is important.** Losing this relationship over commission percentage is disproportionate. Commission math is $5-10k/year range, Medtech's strategic value is multi-year and multi-product (Capture first, then future ClinicPro tools on the same rails).
- **Two contracts, two commercial logics.** Integration contract = commission on direct sales, covers NZ and AU. AU bundle deal = Medtech pays Ryo per bundled practice under Evolution AU launch. Bundle practices and direct-sales practices are largely distinct sets. Both land Wednesday.
- **Wed meeting will discuss both deals together.** Alex and Lawrence both present. Expect attempted linkage. Refuse.

## 3. Commercial model: commission structure

**Why commission, not flat fee:**

- ClinicPro retail prices NZ: $299 / $799 / $1,500 / contact by enrolled patients.
- Medtech's $10-$60/month flat fee = $120-$720/practice/year = ~40% of gross at lowest tier, ~48% at top tier.
- 15% commission = $45 / $120 / $225 per practice per year = 15% flat of gross.
- Commission scales with what ClinicPro can afford to pay. Flat fee does not.

**Illustrative portfolio math (50 NZ practices, mixed tiers):**

Assumed portfolio: 25 × $299 + 15 × $799 + 10 × $1,500 = $34,460 gross.

| Model | Medtech take (NZD/yr) | Ryo retained (NZD/yr) | Ryo retained % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Medtech's flat fee draft ($10-$60/mo) | ~15,600 | ~18,860 | ~55% |
| Ryo's 15% commission counter | ~5,170 | ~29,290 | ~85% |
| Hybrid: 20% commission (fallback) | ~6,890 | ~27,570 | ~80% |

**3x gap between Ryo's 15% ask and Medtech's flat fee take.** Medtech will push back. Fallback at 20% narrows the gap to ~2.3x. Below 20% the margin does not justify the partnership economics.

## 4. Pricing strategy (integration contract)

- **Opening ask**: 15% flat commission on Gross Revenue, both NZ and AU direct sales.
- **Fallback**: 20% flat commission.
- **Walkaway**: above 20%.
- **Do NOT offer** the split 10% self-referred / 15% Medtech-referred structure. Earlier counter, now superseded. Flat 15% is simpler and Alex is already sympathetic.

**Why 15% is defensible:**

- Industry partnership norms: Epic App Orchard took 15-30% on partner apps. 15% sits at the generous end for a partner relationship.
- Alex's personal signal: 30% "too much internally". 15% clears that bar.
- Medtech-side benefit: revenue with zero customer acquisition cost. ClinicPro does all direct-sales work.

**Framing to Alex and the team:**

- ClinicPro does all customer acquisition for direct sales.
- Medtech's 15% is integration access fee + marketing halo, not distribution cost.
- Flat per-facility fee was designed for bundled distribution (Medtech pushes the product), not direct sales (ClinicPro pushes the product). Wrong instrument for this model.

## 5. NZ retail pricing (own side)

**Current**: $299 / $799 / $1,500 / contact by enrolled patients tier.

**Fallback if tier system is operationally too complex to administer under the commission contract:**

- Flat rate at a middle number (e.g., $799/year) for all practices within normal usage.
- Enterprise tier for heavy usage (derm clinics, mega-clinics) exceeding a usage limit.

This is ClinicPro's retail-side fallback, not a Medtech-facing concession. Only relevant if tier enforcement via commission reporting becomes painful in practice.

## 6. Non-negotiable contract clauses

1. **Geographic scope explicit**. Covers both NZ and AU direct sales. Not implied, not NZ-only. **Revised from 14 April position.**
2. **Double-dipping carve-out**. Bundled practices under the AU bundle deal are excluded from commission scope until they convert to direct. Prevents Medtech being paid twice for the same practice. Belt-and-suspenders given bundle and direct are largely distinct sets, but must be explicit.
3. **WHT on AU-sourced commission**. Services fee restructure discussed for AU bundle should extend here. Net outcome: ClinicPro receives 85% (or 80% fallback) of Gross Revenue net of tax.
4. **Scope locked to ClinicPro Capture**. Future ClinicPro tools (Inbox Helper, Care Gap Finder, other modules) require separate commercial agreements.
5. **No exclusivity on ClinicPro's side**. Freedom to distribute through other channels.
6. **Termination + integration IP**. Minimum 6-month wind-down with support obligations if Medtech is acquired or changes direction. Integration-side IP stays with ClinicPro.
7. **PIA/pentest timeline at 6 months from go-live**, not 3 (per original January discussion with Alex).

## 7. Strategic risks to manage

**Risk: Deal linkage with AU bundle.** Medtech's team may trade NZ commission rate for AU bundle price, or push unified contract. Refuse. Two commercial logics, two contracts. Repeat as needed.

**Risk: Flat-fee persistence.** Medtech's team may push flat fee in a different wrapper (revenue minimums, per-facility floor, hybrid). Hold line on pure percentage commission.

**Risk: Slow-walk.** Medtech silent since 14 April. May stall contract while bundle deal closes. Counter-pressure: bundle timeline (Evolution AU G2M May-June) needs integration contract signed cleanly.

**Risk: Broad language drift.** "All current and future ClinicPro software" could creep in. Lock scope to Capture only.

**Risk: Geographic ambiguity.** Current draft is implicit NZ. Must be explicit NZ + AU direct sales, with AU bundle carve-out clearly stated.

**Risk: Commission reporting burden.** Administering 15% commission across NZ and AU retail adds operational overhead. Quarterly reporting is reasonable. Monthly reporting is burdensome and should be refused.
