## Problem

Three marketing claims on the `/referral-images` landing page describe a "full audit trail":

- `app/(marketing)/referral-images/page.tsx` L700
- `app/(marketing)/referral-images/page.tsx` L790
- `app/(marketing)/referral-images/page.tsx` L871

The implementation cannot answer the core audit question: **which images were emailed to which admin?**

Current state:
- `/api/referral-images/email-to-admin/route.ts` logs `value: emailsSent` (a count) into `referral_images_usage_events`, but never persists `recipientEmail` or `imageIds`.
- The download endpoint is not logged at all.
- `medtech_image_commit_audit` table exists in `db/schema/medtech.ts` but is unused.

This is a compliance-adjacent marketing claim not backed by the code. Commercial risk if a practice or auditor asks for a record of who received which images.

## Options

**Option 1 — Back the claim (~1 day effort)**

Extend `referral_images_usage_events` with nullable `recipient_email` (text) and `image_ids` (json array) columns, OR start writing to `medtech_image_commit_audit` per-image per-send.

Add corresponding logging to:
- `/api/referral-images/email-to-admin/route.ts` — capture `recipientEmail` and array of `imageIds` on every send
- The download endpoint — log per-image download events with `userId` and `imageId`

**Option 2 — Soften the copy (~15 min effort)**

Replace "full audit trail" with "usage analytics" or "per-event logging" at the three locations listed above.

## Recommendation

Soften first (same day). Back properly next sprint. Marketing accuracy before product investment.

- `app/(marketing)/referral-images/page.tsx` — L700, L790, L871 (copy change)

- `db/schema/referral-images.ts` — `referral_images_usage_events` table definition
- `db/schema/medtech.ts` — `medtech_image_commit_audit` table (currently unused)
