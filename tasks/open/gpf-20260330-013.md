Complete before the visit date is confirmed. Assessor does a spot check of equipment and will ask about practice systems.

Equipment — check all items are present, calibrated, and in-date:
- Auriscope, ophthalmoscope, sphygmomanometer (calibrated within last year if aneroid)
- Glucometer (calibration strip number must match)
- Peak flow meter, urine dipstick (check expiry)
- Visual acuity charts, weight scales (adult + paediatric)
- Emergency bag: airways, Ambu bag, IV equipment, saline, tourniquet, oxygen
- Emergency drugs: adrenaline 1/1000, atropine, naloxone, GTN, diazepam, glucagon, penicillin + alternative, etc.

Practice systems — know these cold:
- Emergency drill (who does what)
- Cold chain compliance documentation
- Controlled drug storage
- Sterilisation procedures
- Medical waste disposal
- Significant events register
- Complaints procedure and timelines
- Safe transfer of care / results inbox management
- After-hours care arrangements

Room setup:
- Extra chair for assessor (positioned to minimise intrusion, not between doctor and patient)
- Chaperone notice visible near examination couch
- Screensaver/password on all terminals
- H&DC rights poster in waiting room
- Print two copies of Declaration of Confidentiality for assessor to sign
- Patient consent forms for each patient (consent for observer)
