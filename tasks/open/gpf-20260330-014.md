The official guidance says: do NOT pre-select specific patients for the assessor — this is obvious to assessors and can trigger a second visit at your expense.

What you CAN do: ask reception to book the usual mix that reflects your normal practice. If your practice has a diverse patient population, ensure the schedule isn't accidentally skewed (e.g., all routine adults). The assessor expects a representative range including at least one long-term care and one acute consultation.

What peers report helps:
- Having Māori patients on the list (cultural competency is assessed hard — a session with no Māori patients removes one natural demonstration opportunity, though it is NOT a formal fail criterion)
- Some paediatric, elderly, and acute presentations reflecting your real caseload
- Do this through normal booking patterns, not explicit selection

Also: inform all staff what the visit is, why it matters, and to make the assessor welcome. Arrange refreshments — assessors sometimes travel far.

Book 6–8 consultations for the first session, plus 2–4 as buffer for the second session. Minimum 10 min each; 15–20 min for complex or examination cases.
